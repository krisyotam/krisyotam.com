## Shakespeare's Sonnets

Shakespeare's sonnets are 154 poems attributed to William Shakespeare which cover themes such as the passage of time, love, beauty and mortality. The quarto of 1609 includes all 154 sonnets.

### Structure

Shakespeare's sonnets follow the structure of the English sonnet:
- 14 lines of iambic pentameter
- A rhyme scheme of ABAB CDCD EFEF GG
- Often divided into three quatrains and a final couplet

### Historical Context

The sonnets were first published in 1609 by Thomas Thorpe, though they were likely written over a period of several years. Many scholars believe some may have been composed in the early 1590s.

### Notable Sonnets

- **Sonnet 18**: "Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?"
- **Sonnet 116**: "Let me not to the marriage of true minds"
- **Sonnet 130**: "My mistress' eyes are nothing like the sun"
