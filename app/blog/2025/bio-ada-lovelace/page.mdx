## Ada Lovelace (1815â€“1852)

- **Born**: December 10, 1815, in London, England.
- **Full Name**: Augusta Ada Byron, later Countess of Lovelace.
- **Parentage**:
  - Daughter of poet Lord Byron and mathematician Annabella Milbanke.
- **Fields of Interest**:
  - Mathematics
  - Early Computing and Analytical Engines

- **Major Contributions**:
  - Worked closely with Charles Babbage on his Analytical Engine.
  - Authored detailed notes describing algorithms to compute Bernoulli numbers, considered the first published computer algorithm.

- **Significance**:
  - Often recognized as the world's first computer programmer.
  - Insightfully envisioned computing machines capable of tasks beyond mere calculation.

- **Legacy**:
  - Ada Lovelace Day celebrates women in STEM.
  - Continues to inspire contributions toward gender equality in technology and science.
