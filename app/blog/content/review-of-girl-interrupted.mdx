# Contextual Framing
 
Girl, Interrupted is a adaption of the 1993 novel of the same name by Susanna Kaysen. It stars Winona Ryder as Kaysen herslef, and Angelina Jolie as the 
mercurial Lisa Rowe. The film was directed by James Mangold, and takes place in 1967 during the Vietnam War era and the counterculture movement in america. 
     
  
# Succinct Plot Setup  

Susanna Kaysen, a 18 year old recent Highschool Graduate chases a bottle of aspirin with a bottle of vodka. She then finds herself in Claymoore Psychiatric Hospital. 
Susanna meets several young women she becomes friends with such as Georgina Tuskin, Lisa Rowe, Daisy Randone on her journey. The story explores the lives and illnesses
of the patients of Claymoore, and Susannas vie for redemption through the outward expression of what she calls "an irrefutably inward condition.". What follows is a 
tried and tested, emotionally rich & complex narrative with loveable & imperfect characters. 
   
  
# Emotional Response 

At the time of this review I have seen girl, interrupted around 4 times. It ages like wine. Each time my attention is further drawn to the minute complextities of another 
character. No detail is forsaken. First I fell in love with Susanna, a character many of us can relate to. One who struggled to convey her own pain to others, as if having a 
heart attack at a concert, so many people around you with no one surrounding you. Next with Lisa a girl diagnosed with Antisocial Personality Disorder (ASPD) she is manipulative, lacks empathy and often remorse, 
frequently disregards authority, and is often prone to aggression. Still she is charming, and charismatic, and though imperfect with a deeply flawed worldview she plays the villan so that no 
one else has to.
