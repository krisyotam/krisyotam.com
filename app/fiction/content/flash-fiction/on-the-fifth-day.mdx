
Every day, hour, minute, and second arrived the same in Thistlewood. A town that survived off itself. Thistlewood was not 
fond of outsiders. Outside events, Outside ideas, Outside anything. Everyone walked the same path, wore the same clothes, 
talked the same talk, and thought the same thoughts. There was no need for change, no room for growth. 

On the first day. The world went grayscale, the skies ashen, the grass muted, the sun lifeless. People moved as if 
nothing had changed. The sun rose and set, the moon waxed and waned, but the colors never renewed their lease. The 
art meseums closed, the artists quit, the musicians stopped playing, and the writers put down thier pens. Photographers 
stopped taking pictures, and their was no more record of the world as it was.
