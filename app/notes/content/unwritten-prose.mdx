# Unwritten Prose

Essays, stories, and pieces that exist fully formed in my mind but haven't made it to the page. Some are waiting for the right moment, others for the courage to write them.

## Essays in Progress

- **On the Art of Productive Procrastination**: How strategic delay can improve creative output
- **The Loneliness of Being Right Too Early**: What it feels like to see trends before they're visible to others
- **In Defense of Changing Your Mind**: Why intellectual consistency is overrated
- **The Attention Economy is a Pyramid Scheme**: How social media monetizes human connection
- **Learning to Think in Systems**: A practical guide to seeing connections everywhere

## Personal Narratives

- **The Year I Said No to Everything**: What happened when I stopped accepting invitations and commitments
- **Conversations with My Future Self**: Letters to who I hope to become
- **The Things I Learned from Failure**: Stories from projects that didn't work but taught me everything
- **On Being Ordinary**: Finding meaning in a world that celebrates exceptional people

## Creative Fiction

- **The Algorithm Whisperer**: Short story about someone who can predict AI behavior through intuition
- **Letters from the Post-Privacy Era**: Correspondence between people who remember when privacy existed
- **The Last Library**: A story about preserving human knowledge in a post-digital world
- **Time Travelers Anonymous**: Support group for people unstuck in time

## Technical Writing

- **The Programmer's Guide to Explaining Things**: How to bridge the gap between technical and non-technical thinking
- **Documentation as Love Letter**: Why good documentation is an act of care for future users
- **The Art of Code Review**: How to give feedback that improves both code and relationships

## Philosophical Explorations

- **The Ethics of Optimization**: When making things better makes them worse
- **On the Nature of Understanding**: The difference between knowing facts and comprehending truth
- **The Responsibility of Intelligence**: What we owe others when we can see further ahead

*Each title represents hours of thinking compressed into a few words. Someday they'll expand back into full pieces.*
