About Me

Greetings! I am Kris Yotam, a classical educator, lifelong learner, and passionate researcher. My journey has been shaped by a relentless curiosity and a commitment to the pursuit of knowledge. I have spent years refining my understanding of various disciplines—from the humanities to technology—and I aim to share these insights through this platform.

As an autodidact and tutor, I believe deeply in the value of self-directed learning and the transformative power of education. My work is an ongoing pursuit of the true, the good, and the beautiful, inspired by classical principles and grounded in practical wisdom.

My Mission

My mission is simple: to guide others toward the true, the good, and the beautiful. In a world that often values the superficial, I advocate for education that shapes the soul, not just the mind. I believe in classical education, which is not only about imparting knowledge but nurturing virtue, wisdom, and a deep understanding of the world around us.

I aim to inspire individuals to think critically, act with purpose, and live according to timeless principles. My work here is not just to teach, but to help others transform their lives through education that leads to flourishing.

Experience

With over a decade of experience in various fields, including teaching, research, and software development, I have cultivated a deep understanding of both classical education and modern methodologies. Below are some of the roles I have undertaken:

  • Classical Educator and Tutor – Educating students in subjects ranging from philosophy and literature to logic and mathematics. I emphasize the development of critical thinking and moral character.
  • Researcher – Engaging in interdisciplinary research that bridges the gap between classical studies and modern thought, particularly in areas related to philosophy, history, and the sciences.
  • Programmer – Applying my analytical skills to the world of technology, where I design and develop software that serves both practical and educational purposes.

Personal Philosophy

At the core of my philosophy is the belief that education should lead to a deeper understanding of God's design for the world. As a believer in the Bible as the ultimate authority, I see my work as a reflection of divine wisdom. I practice asceticism and minimalism, aiming to live simply and focus on what is truly important—knowledge, virtue, and faith.

I view education as a tool for moral and intellectual transformation, with the goal of shaping individuals who can contribute meaningfully to society while living lives of integrity, humility, and wisdom.

Core Values

The foundation of my work and my life is built upon the following values, which guide all of my decisions and interactions:

  • Mitzvah – I aspire to be blameless and keep the 613 laws, statues, and commandments of the Most High God YHWH
  • Minimalism – Concious and intentional practice of living with only what is essential.
  • Integrity – I hold honesty and transparency in the highest regard. Trust and authenticity are the cornerstones of my relationships.
  • Asceticism – Self-discipline through renunciation.
  • Nekkhamma – The cessation of craving/desire.
  • Stewardship – Extreme responsibility, as the earth does not only belong to us but the future generations.
  • Contemplation – The dedicated practice of deep and reflective thought, often involving meditation, and introspection.
  • Service – Compassionate commitment to helping others, expressed through heart, and action.
  • Simplicity – As simple as possible, not simpler.
  • Brevity – Conciseness and succinctness in expression.
